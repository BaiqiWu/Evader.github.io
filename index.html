Evader is a query-free black-box attack that subtly modifies AI-generated images through an adversarial conditional diffusion plus semantic attention dispersion and frequency feature augmentation, allowing them to bypass a wide range of forensic detectors while remaining visually indistinguishable from the originals.

Abstract

The rapid advancement of AI-generated images (AIGI), particularly deepfakes, has raised significant concerns about digital security, privacy, and the dissemination of misinformation. However, despite the promising potential of numerous forensic detectors, their inherent reliance on classifiers makes them vulnerable to adversarial attacks. While countermeasures have been proposed to enhance robustness, we propose a novel adversarial conditional diffusion-based attack, Evader, designed to bypass these advanced detectors. Evader achieves superior effectiveness, transferability, and imperceptibility by leveraging semantic attention dispersion and frequency swapping, and by employing a diffusion-constraint strategy to regulate the adversarial trajectory. To facilitate fair comparison, we have built the first large-scale robust forensic detection benchmark, comprising nine generated datasets, twelve detectors, ten attack methods, and three adversarial defense methods. Extensive experiments demonstrate that Evader not only outperforms existing advanced attack methods but also successfully bypasses various state-of-the-art academic detection algorithms and commercial detectors, achieving an average transfer attack success rate of 84.03%. These results raise significant security and privacy concerns. The code is available in the submitted artifacts.
